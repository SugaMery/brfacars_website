Why Choose Car Rental at Casablanca Airport?

Casablanca is Morocco's economic and cultural hub, and renting a car at the airport ensures you can start your journey as soon as you arrive. Here’s why renting a car at Casablanca Airport is a great choice:

  • Convenient Pick-Up: Skip the hassle of taxis or shuttle services and pick up your rental car directly from the airport.
  • Explore Casablanca at Your Own Pace: Discover the vibrant city of Casablanca, from its modern architecture to its historic sites, without relying on public transport.
  • Affordable Rates: With BrfaCars, you can find rental cars at competitive prices, making it an affordable way to explore Morocco.
  • Variety of Vehicles: Whether you're looking for a compact car, a luxury vehicle, or a spacious SUV, Casablanca Airport offers a wide range of rental options to suit your needs.

How to Rent a Car at Casablanca Airport

Renting a car at Casablanca Airport is a simple and straightforward process. Follow these steps to get started:

  1. Visit BrfaCars website to view available rental options at Casablanca Mohammed V Airport.
  2. Select your desired vehicle, rental dates, and pick-up location at the airport.
  3. Complete your booking online and receive a confirmation email with all your reservation details.
  4. Upon arrival at Casablanca Airport, head to the BrfaCars counter or pick-up location to collect your car.

Documents Required for Car Rental at Casablanca Airport

To rent a car at Casablanca Airport, you will need the following documents:

  • A valid driver’s license (an International Driving Permit may be required for non-Moroccan residents).
  • A credit card for payment and security deposit.
  • Your passport or national ID for identification purposes.

Top Attractions in Casablanca to Explore by Car

With your rental car, you can easily explore the best attractions in Casablanca. Here are some top places to visit:

  • Hassan II Mosque: One of the largest mosques in the world, with stunning architecture and views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Old Medina: A historical area filled with traditional souks, restaurants, and shops.
  • Royal Palace of Casablanca: A beautiful example of Moroccan architecture and culture.
  • Ain Diab Beach: Relax on the beach or enjoy a variety of water sports.
  • The Corniche: A popular promenade along the coast with cafes and restaurants offering sea views.
